Research and Projects

Explore my research work in the fields of Machine Learning, AI, and Web Development.

Automatic threat detection using URL classification techniques

Tools & Technologies: Machine Learning (ML), Deep Learning (DL)

Summary: The paper discussed various machine learning approaches, including artificial neural networks, adaptive boosting, decision trees, random forest regression, and principal component analysis, to detect security flaws such as SQL injection, Cross-Site Scripting, malicious URLs, phishing attacks, path traversal, and shell injection.

Publication: This paper was selected for the International Conference on Intelligent Technologies (CONIT, 2022) and published in IEEE Xplore.

Calculating drought probabilities across sub-divisions of India

Tools & Technologies: Python, Matplotlib

Summary: Analyzed annual precipitation data from 1901 to 2017, sourced from the National Data Sharing and Accessibility Policy (NDSAP) to predict drought probabilities across 36 sub-divisions of India.

Award: This research won the Best Presentation Award at the Multidisciplinary Conference on Engineering Science & Technology (MCEST, 2022) in the AI/ML and Data Science track.

AutoVaidya-AI based diagnosis of Invasive Ductal Carcinoma

Tools & Technologies: Tensorflow, Keras

Summary: As a part of Wootech mentorship program 2020, I developed an image classification model to differentiate between histopathology scans of normal lymph node tissues and metastatic tissues for the early detection of IDC.

Validation: Performed rigorous validation to ensure the model could adapt across different datasets, ensuring its dependability in actual clinical settings.

Automatic image captioning

Tools & Technologies: ResNet50model, LSTM, Keras, Numpy

Summary: The image is sent into CNN, which recognizes the activity in the image and creates a vector representation, which is then fed into the LSTM model, which generates a word and a caption. I built a multimodal neural network that uses feature vectors generated by both RNN and CNN, which means we'll need two inputs. The first is the image we need to describe, which serves as a feed to the CNN, and the second is the words in the text sequence that have been produced thus far as a sequence, which serves as the RNN's input.

Anxidote-Website to predict anxiety & depression levels

Tools & Technologies: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Machine learning (ML)

Summary: We have designed a website in which all a person needs to do is take our survey which has questions taken from DASS’21 research paper. On the basis of the formula given, our application calculates a score according to the answers selected by the people. Our Machine Learning model is trained on a dataset using an algorithm to predict the level of anxiety the person is suffering with. Our website will serve as a platform to raise awareness about anxiety issues and provide helpful resources to those who need it.
